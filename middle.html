Middle Tunisia

The middle of Tunisia, often characterized by its semi-arid landscapes and historic significance, holds a captivating allure. Dominated by vast stretches of Saharan desert and rugged terrain, this region boasts a timeless charm that echoes with tales of ancient civilizations and cultural exchanges.

01

Tunisia encompasses the Atlas Mountains, which extend across North Africa. These mountains not only provide breathtaking scenery but also serve as a natural barrier between the coastal regions and the Sahara Desert, influencing the climate and biodiversity of the area.

02

The city of Kairouan, considered one of the holiest cities in Islam after Mecca, Medina, and Jerusalem. Kairouan is renowned for its Great Mosque, one of the oldest and most important mosques in the Islamic world, as well as its rich history as a center of Islamic scholarship and culture.
